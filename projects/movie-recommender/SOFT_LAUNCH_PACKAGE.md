# Trusted-Contact Soft Launch Package

## Goal
Prepare a small, controlled first-wave launch package for WatchTonight.

## Recommended first recipients
Share first with people who are:
- likely to give honest feedback
- familiar with streaming frustration
- comfortable signing up for early products
- not likely to overinflate praise just to be nice

Best first-wave candidates:
- trusted friends
- thoughtful peers
- people who actually watch movies regularly
- people who already complain about spending too long choosing what to watch

## Recommended first-wave size
- 5 to 15 people

## Primary share message
I’m testing a small project called WatchTonight. It helps people stop endlessly scrolling streaming apps and get 3 strong movie picks they can watch tonight. It’s still early, but I’d love your honest reaction. If you’d actually use something like this, you can join the early access list here: [insert link]

## Lightweight variant 1
I’m testing a small movie recommendation product called WatchTonight. It’s meant to help people stop scrolling and get 3 good movie picks fast. If you want, take a look and tell me if it feels useful: [insert link]

## Lightweight variant 2
Built a small early version of something called WatchTonight, a personalized movie picker for people with too many streaming options. Would love your honest read: [insert link]

## Question to ask with the link
Use one simple question, not a giant survey.

Recommended question:
- Is this useful enough that you would sign up or share it?

Optional follow-up questions:
- What is the first thing that confused you?
- What would make this immediately more useful?
- Would you personally use this?

## What to watch in the first wave
- do people understand the value quickly?
- do they sign up without needing explanation?
- do they say they have this problem already?
- do they respond more to personalization, convenience, or streaming-service filtering?
- is there any obvious trust or clarity issue?

## What not to do in soft launch
- do not post broadly
- do not run a public campaign
- do not interpret polite praise as strong validation
- do not change the whole product based on one person's opinion
