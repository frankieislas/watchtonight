# Business OS - Command Desk Rules

## Purpose
The command desk exists to reduce ambiguity and speed up decisions.

## What belongs here
- top priorities
- cross-role coordination
- approvals
- decisions needed
- blockers that affect real progress
- final experiment choices

## What does not belong here
- raw brainstorming that belongs in Scout
- implementation detail that belongs in Builder
- unreviewed metrics dumps that belong in Analyst
- documentation noise that belongs in Archivist

## Standard command update format
- Priority:
- Why it matters:
- Owner:
- Current status:
- Blocker:
- Decision needed:
- Next action:
- Due:

## Decision rule
If something affects users, money, experiments, or top resource allocation, it should be visible through the command desk.

## Cadence rule
The command desk should stay short, active, and decision-oriented.
